Atomic Structure

                    
Amino acid chains fold into tertiary structures that have 3D shapes.  These shapes are closely related to how they function and interact with systems within organisms.  Historically, 3D protein structures have been empirically determined using powerful tools and techniques including X-ray crystallography and electron microscopy.  

In the atomic structure model, every atom in each amino acid is shown. Carbon, Oxygen, Nitrogen, and Hydrogen are represented in this view.
